=== 英译中 ===
译文: Here are a few options for translating "Hello, how are you?" into Chinese, depending on the level of formality:

**1. Most Common & Versatile:**

*   **你好，你怎么样？ (Nǐ hǎo, nǐ zěnme yàng?)**
    *   你好 (Nǐ hǎo) - Hello
    *   你怎么样？(nǐ zěnme yàng?) - How are you? (This is a very common and natural way to ask)

**2. Slightly More Formal:**

*   **您好，您最近怎么样？ (Nín hǎo, nín zuìjìn zěnme yàng?)**
    *   您好 (Nín hǎo) - Hello (using "您" which is the polite form of "you")
    *   您最近怎么样？(Nín zuìjìn zěnme yàng?) - How have you been recently? (More polite and suitable for elders or superiors)

**3.  Very Casual:**

*   **嗨，你最近好吗？ (Hā, nǐ zuìjìn hǎo ma?)**
    *   嗨 (Hā) - Hi (borrowed from English)
    *   你最近好吗？(nǐ zuìjìn hǎo ma?) - Are you doing well recently? (More casual and friendly)

**Explanation of Choices:**

*   **您 (Nín)** is the polite form of "you."  Use it when speaking to elders, superiors, or people you don't know well.
*   **你 (Nǐ)** is the standard "you" and is appropriate for most everyday conversations.
*   **怎么样 (zěnme yàng)**  is a general way to ask "how are you?"
*   **最近 (zuìjìn)** means "recently" or "lately," adding a sense of asking about their current state.

**Recommendation:**

For most situations, **你好，你怎么样？ (Nǐ hǎo, nǐ zěnme yàng?)** is the best and most versatile option.

=== 中译英 ===
译文: Here are a few options for translating "今天天气真好，我们出去走走吧。" into English, with slightly different nuances:

*   **"The weather is really nice today. Let's go for a walk."** (This is the most common and natural translation.)
*   **"It's such a beautiful day today. Why don’t we go for a walk?"** (This adds a bit more enthusiasm.)
*   **"The weather is lovely today. How about we go out for a stroll?"** (This uses slightly more formal language.)

The best option depends on the context and how you want to convey the feeling. However, **"The weather is really nice today. Let's go for a walk."** is generally the most suitable and widely understood translation.
